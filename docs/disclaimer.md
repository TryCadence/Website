# Disclaimer

## Important Notice

Cadence is a tool designed to **assist** in detecting AI generated content, but it is **not perfect** and should not be treated as an infallible authority. AI detection is an inherently challenging problem, and no tool including Cadence can guarantee 100% accuracy.

## Limitations

### False Positives
Cadence may occasionally flag content as AI-generated when it was actually written by a human. This can happen because:

- Some human writing styles naturally exhibit patterns similar to AI-generated text
- Technical documentation often uses formulaic language that triggers detection
- Boilerplate code and common programming patterns may be flagged
- Non-native English speakers may have writing patterns that trigger false positives

### False Negatives
Cadence may also miss AI-generated content. This can occur because:

- AI models are constantly evolving and improving
- Some AI-generated content is heavily edited by humans
- New AI writing styles may not match existing detection patterns
- Sophisticated prompt engineering can produce more "human-like" output

### Confidence Scores Are Estimates
The confidence scores provided by Cadence are **probabilistic estimates**, not certainties. A high confidence score suggests a higher likelihood of AI-generated content, but it does not constitute proof.

## Recommended Use

### What Cadence Is Good For

- **Initial screening** of large codebases or content libraries
- **Flagging suspicious content** for human review
- **Trend analysis** across repositories over time
- **Awareness** of potential AI-assisted contributions
- **Educational purposes** to understand AI writing patterns

### What Cadence Is NOT

- A definitive proof that content is AI-generated
- A replacement for human judgment and review
- A tool for making accusations without further investigation
- A legally binding verification system
